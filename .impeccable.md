## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are event participants, collaborators, mentors, and demo viewers at a vibe-coding event. They will see the product in a live setting where attention is fragmented, time is short, and the first impression must communicate that the prototype is alive, voice-capable, and ready to be extended.

The immediate job is not long-term product operation. The job is to make a working ElevenLabs-powered idea legible in seconds, then leave enough control and status visibility for the builder to keep iterating during the event.

### Brand Personality

Three-word personality: **stage-ready, tactile, improvisational**.

The interface should feel like a live instrument or production desk: confident, warm, slightly performative, and ready for audio interaction. It should not feel like a generic SaaS dashboard, crypto landing page, AI chatbot template, or beige productivity tool.

### Aesthetic Direction

Visual tone: editorial live-demo console. Light-first, with deep ink, warm paper, signal green, and amber accent. Use strong type, deliberate asymmetry, and status surfaces that feel operational without becoming dense enterprise UI.

The first screen should communicate:

- this is a live demo workspace;
- ElevenLabs integration is expected;
- local server and tunnel readiness matter;
- the project can quickly become a voice/audio prototype.

Avoid purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, generic icon cards, oversized marketing fluff, and decorative code-terminal tropes.

### Design Principles

1. **Make demo readiness visible.** Surface runtime, tunnel, and ElevenLabs readiness plainly.
2. **Keep the screen performative, not promotional.** This is a working stage, not a landing page.
3. **Use warmth with control.** Pair tactile editorial styling with precise status affordances.
4. **Design for interruption.** A viewer should understand the state after a quick glance.
5. **Preserve event speed.** Polish should not introduce heavy dependencies or fragile setup.
